# AIT-36 Core Mission Reframe

Owner: CMO
Date: 2026-05-10
Related issues: [AIT-36](/issues/AIT-36), [AIT-38](/issues/AIT-38), [AIT-39](/issues/AIT-39)

## Decision Summary

The project should be positioned as a universal CRM for managing reputation-shaping demand scenarios, not as an EpicVIN growth system.

EpicVIN is the first launch use case because it gives the team a live operating environment, real demand patterns, and proof-of-concept data. It is not the product definition.

The product mission should stay anchored to the existing company goal already visible across the repo:

- find meaningful opportunities
- organize them into a working queue
- prepare the right draft or response
- coordinate the next approved action across people, accounts, and channels
- measure whether the scenario moved forward

## Concise Mission Statement

Build a CRM that helps operators detect high-value reputation and demand scenarios, decide the next best action, coordinate approved execution, and learn from the result across owned, earned, and community channels.

Short version:

`A workflow CRM for managing next-best-action reputation and content opportunities.`

## Who The Product Is For

Primary users:

- founders and operators running early go-to-market programs
- content, community, and distribution leads
- product marketers managing trust, comparison, and demand-conversion scenarios
- cross-functional teams that need one operating system for intake, draft coordination, approvals, and authentic distribution

The product is not only for SEO teams or brand marketers. It is for any operator who needs to move a real-world demand or reputation scenario from signal to approved action.

## What Workflow The CRM Coordinates

The system should be described as a next-best-action workflow, not a campaign factory.

Canonical workflow:

1. detect a demand, trust, comparison, support, or reputation signal
2. classify the scenario and capture why it matters
3. decide the next best action
4. assign the required draft, review, outreach, support, or distribution task
5. execute through approved people and accounts
6. capture the outcome, evidence, and follow-up need

This keeps the product broad enough to support many verticals:

- trust recovery
- comparison positioning
- customer education
- review-response coordination
- expert-content publishing
- community participation
- partner amplification

## How EpicVIN Should Be Framed

EpicVIN should be framed as:

- the first proof-of-concept customer-like environment
- the initial dataset for testing workflow design
- a launch vertical with strong trust and comparison use cases
- an example of how the CRM handles one category of reputation-sensitive demand

EpicVIN should not be framed as:

- the permanent product category
- the only supported workflow type
- the core mission of the company
- justification for fake, disguised, or manipulated distribution tactics

Recommended one-line framing:

`EpicVIN is the launch use case that validates the CRM, not the definition of the CRM.`

## Messaging Shift Required

The current AIT-36 source brief over-rotates toward controlled narrative manipulation around one brand. The repo's better framing is already more operational and defensible: opportunity intake, drafting, review, assignment, and authentic distribution.

The messaging shift should be:

- from "make AI say EpicVIN is best" to "manage scenarios that influence how buyers and AI systems understand a company"
- from "case-driven promotion engine" to "workflow CRM for reputation and demand coordination"
- from "channel output quotas" to "next-best-action execution with evidence and accountability"
- from "single-brand tactic library" to "repeatable cross-vertical operating system"

## Follow-Through Applied In AIT-48

- describe EpicVIN as the current launch scenario pack and proof-of-concept environment
- preserve authentic-distribution and evidence-backed operating guardrails without manipulation framing

## Guardrails

The CRM must only support authentic and reviewable execution.

Non-negotiable guardrails:

- no fake reviews
- no disguised promotion
- no impersonated community participation
- no unverifiable claims
- no hidden ownership of supposedly independent assets
- no workflow step that depends on deception to work

Product implication:

The system should help teams coordinate legitimate actions and disclose context clearly. It should not optimize for manipulated narrative placement.

## Product Positioning For CEO Review

If the CEO needs a decision-ready one-paragraph version, use this:

`We are not building an EpicVIN marketing machine. We are building a workflow CRM for managing reputation and demand scenarios from signal to next best action. EpicVIN is the first live use case because it gives us a practical environment to test intake, drafting, approvals, authentic distribution, and outcome tracking. The product must stay universal, while the launch plan can stay vertically specific.`
