# Weekly GTM Scorecard

Owner: CMO
Review cadence: Monday planning, Wednesday risk check, Friday CEO review
Created: 2026-05-10
Current scorecard week: 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-15

## Purpose

This is the compact weekly scorecard for the current go-to-market operating loop.

It exists to answer four questions every week:

- are enough qualified opportunities entering the queue
- are drafts moving fast enough to create publishable output
- are distribution assignments getting completed inside the planned week
- are the four priority demand scenarios receiving real coverage

Keep the scorecard small until a dedicated content/distribution operator exists.

## Metric Definitions

| Metric | Owner | Weekly target | Update point |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Qualified opportunities added | CMO | 10+ | Monday and Friday |
| Opportunity triage SLA | CMO | 90% triaged within 2 business days | Wednesday and Friday |
| Drafts moved to ready for distribution | CMO | 3+ once workflow is live | Wednesday and Friday |
| Opportunity-to-draft median cycle time | CMO | Under 7 calendar days | Friday |
| Distribution completion rate | CMO | 85%+ of assigned tasks completed in week | Wednesday and Friday |
| Active approved-account participation | CMO | 5+ participating accounts or employees once live | Friday |
| Scenario coverage | CMO | Each priority scenario touched at least once per week | Friday |
| Publishable assets or credible mention opportunities advanced | CMO | 2+ per week once workflow is live | Friday |

## Status Key

- green: on target with no material execution risk this week
- yellow: below target or likely to miss without intervention
- red: materially off target or blocked by missing ownership/capacity

## Operating Rules

- Do not invent precision. If a metric is not yet measured, mark it red and state what needs to be tracked.
- Keep this scorecard to one page of real signals plus short notes.
- Update in place each week and preserve prior weeks below this section if the file stays readable.
- If a metric misses because no owner exists, call that out directly instead of hiding it in narrative.
