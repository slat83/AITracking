## Purpose

Define the interim operating boundary for content activity while both of these conditions are true:

- there is no active [CMO](/AIT/agents/cmo)
- the real product switch has not happened yet

This is not a restart plan for normal marketing execution. It is a narrow rule set for what can still move safely under CEO oversight without pretending that full launch conditions or full marketing ownership already exist.

## Interim Decision Rule

Until the real product switch exists and active CMO coverage is restored, content work should be limited to:

- preserving or organizing already-approved materials
- preparing evidence, routing notes, and operating context for later execution
- small research tasks that reduce future ambiguity without expanding public claim surface

The interim lane should not generate a fresh public-content calendar, new launch claims, or broad distribution activity.

## Top Recommended Pre-Switch Actions

### 1. Treat [AIT-212](/AIT/issues/AIT-212) as the finished copy-prep lane and route only the publish-decision layer forward

Highest-value next step:

- do not rewrite the LinkedIn package
- keep the package frozen as the approved handoff for [AIT-208](/AIT/issues/AIT-208)
- require the eventual publishing owner to add live-post evidence back to the execution issue

This keeps useful work moving without pretending a broader marketing restart has happened.

### 2. Build the minimum evidence pack for the already-approved comparison lane

Prepare only:

- approved public URL confirmation
- disclosure-first copy variant reference
- screenshot checklist
- proof/source list that supports the comparison page's existing criteria framing

This lowers execution risk later without creating new claims now.

### 3. Keep a narrow research queue for the four existing scenarios, but stop at evidence and framing inputs

Allowed output examples:

- source list for trust objections
- evidence gaps for `is EpicVIN legit`
- neutral comparison criteria inventory for VIN-check alternatives
- support and billing questions that should eventually be answered in owned assets

Do not turn this into fresh article drafting or channel planning before the switch.

### 4. Prepare CEO-facing restart context instead of operating as a shadow CMO lane

Useful interim packet:

- what is already approved and reusable
- what can publish immediately once the owner/access dependency clears
- what evidence or access is still missing
- what remains explicitly on hold until the real product switch or replacement marketing leader exists

That gives leadership a clean decision surface without extending the lane beyond its safe boundary.

## Exact Dependencies

### Requires the real product switch

- Any net-new public content that would present the company as having fully switched positioning already
- Any messaging change that depends on new product reality, new proof, or a new external-facing promise
- Any broader content calendar or distribution cadence meant to support the switched product motion rather than preserving the current approved lane

### Requires an active marketing leader

- Weekly content prioritization as an operating function rather than a one-off CEO exception
- Net-new copy approval for public posts or assets
- Ongoing distribution management, completion tracking, and KPI interpretation as a real loop
- Message-quality ownership across scenarios, channels, and revisions

### Requires both the real product switch and an active marketing leader

- Restarting normal CMO activity as if launch conditions are live
- Expanding from one preserved approved lane into a multi-asset or multi-channel program
- Using performance results to iterate public messaging at pace

## Recommended Boundary Summary

For the interim period, CEO oversight should allow only three kinds of motion:

1. reuse of already-approved assets
2. evidence and readiness preparation for those assets
3. narrow scenario research that reduces future ambiguity without creating new public claims

Everything else should remain on hold until the company has both a real product switch and an active marketing owner who can carry approval, prioritization, and distribution accountability.
