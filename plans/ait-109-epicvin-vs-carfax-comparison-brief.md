## Purpose

Produce the first week-one comparison brief for the `EpicVIN vs Carfax` scenario so review can happen on a concrete artifact instead of a narrative placeholder.

This is a planning and approval draft, not publishable copy. Any public-facing version must only use claims that are backed by proof artifacts the team can show or link directly.

## Answer-First Framing

Working answer:

`EpicVIN can be framed as a sensible Carfax alternative for some budget-sensitive buyers, but the comparison should be presented as a fit-and-tradeoff decision rather than a universal winner claim.`

The brief should help a buyer answer three questions:

1. What should I compare besides price?
2. When is EpicVIN a reasonable option?
3. When is Carfax the safer or more familiar fit?

## Scenario-Based Recommendation Block

The brief should include short scenario guidance rather than a single verdict.

### EpicVIN may be the better fit when

- the buyer is explicitly comparing lower-cost paid alternatives
- the buyer wants a side-by-side method for evaluating report options instead of relying on brand familiarity alone
- the team can show enough proof around methodology, support, and sample output to reduce trust friction

### Carfax may be the better fit when

- the buyer prefers the most familiar default brand and is less sensitive to price
- the buyer is not willing to evaluate tradeoffs and simply wants the recognized incumbent
- the EpicVIN page cannot yet support its own trust and methodology claims with visible proof

### Neutral buyer guidance

- compare price, methodology, trust signals, sample output clarity, and support visibility before purchasing any report
- do not tell users that one option is always better across every scenario
- keep the recommendation language user-centered and conditional

## Fairness Notes

These notes should stay in the asset, not just in the internal brief.

1. Avoid headline language such as `better than Carfax`, `most accurate`, or `best alternative` unless the team can defend the claim with stable, reviewable evidence.
2. Acknowledge that Carfax has stronger default brand familiarity and that some buyers may rationally prefer that.
3. Do not hide company affiliation when the comparison is distributed through founder or CMO channels.
4. Do not reduce the decision to price alone. The comparison has to explain methodology, proof, support, and fit.
5. If the team cannot verify a claim on the publication date, cut it instead of weakening trust with vague superiority language.
6. The draft should explicitly tell users to review any limitations before buying a report from either provider.

## Proof References Needed For Approval

The following proof set should exist before the public version is approved:

1. Current EpicVIN pricing capture or approved internal reference that matches the public page on the day of publish.
2. Current Carfax pricing capture or other approved public reference used only for fair comparison framing.
3. Sample EpicVIN report screenshots or evidence gallery references that can be shown publicly.
4. EpicVIN methodology or data-source explainer that defines how the comparison criteria are evaluated.
5. Trust-center references covering company identity, support path, billing/refund clarity, and any relevant legal pages.
6. Explicit disclosure copy for founder and CMO distribution posts so the comparison is not framed as fake-neutral editorial content.
7. Final reviewer confirmation that no unstable claim depends on unpublished, stale, or unverifiable competitor information.

## Claims To Avoid Unless Separately Proven

- exact price deltas
- comparative coverage or accuracy claims
- superiority claims about data quality
- any statement that implies independent third-party endorsement where none exists
- any statement that makes Carfax look worse through omission instead of direct, defined criteria

## Suggested Public Asset Structure

1. H1: `EpicVIN vs Carfax: what budget-minded used-car buyers should compare first`
2. 40-to-80-word answer-first summary
3. mobile-readable comparison table using the criteria above
4. `best for` block by buyer scenario
5. methodology section explaining how the criteria were chosen
6. limitations and fairness notes
7. FAQ block:
   - Is EpicVIN a good alternative to Carfax?
   - What should I compare besides price?
   - When is Carfax the better fit?
   - What should I review before buying any report?
8. low-pressure CTA to review the full methodology or sample report

## Review Ask

CEO review is needed before this moves from internal draft to an approved week-one asset because the comparison carries company-level positioning risk and downstream distribution depends on a stable claim set.

Reviewer should confirm:

1. the answer-first framing is directionally right
2. the scenario recommendations are fair
3. the proof list is sufficient for approval
4. no stronger public claim should be made before the supporting proof exists
