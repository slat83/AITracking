## Target Framing

### Founder LinkedIn account (CEO-owned)

Use when the approved brief is ready and the team wants a founder-voice take on buyer tradeoffs.

Message angle:

- frame the post as a practical buying note for used-car shoppers comparing a known premium default with a lower-cost alternative
- emphasize when a buyer should compare tools instead of assuming one service is always the best fit
- keep the voice operator-level and transparent rather than promotional

What the founder post should try to do:

- signal that EpicVIN can be a credible option in some budget-sensitive scenarios
- acknowledge that buyers should check methodology, coverage, and use-case fit before paying
- send readers to the approved comparison asset for the full criteria table and fairness notes

### CMO LinkedIn account

Use when the approved brief is ready and the team wants a methodology-led summary from the operator responsible for GTM quality.

Message angle:

- summarize the comparison framework itself
- highlight the decision criteria buyers should use when evaluating EpicVIN and Carfax
- reinforce that the linked asset explains tradeoffs rather than pushing a blanket winner claim

What the CMO post should try to do:

- make the methodology look credible and reusable
- support the founder post with a more explicit criteria-driven explanation
- create a second disclosed distribution touchpoint without changing the factual claim set

## Packaging Notes

### Shared package for both posts

- link only to the approved `EpicVIN vs Carfax` asset, not to a broader homepage or unrelated sales page
- use one stable claim set taken directly from the approved brief
- keep screenshots optional for LinkedIn unless the final asset ships with a clean mobile-readable comparison table
- avoid "best", "most accurate", or "better than Carfax" language unless the approved asset explicitly supports it with methodology

### Founder post packaging

- ideal format: short text post with one practical buyer takeaway and a single link
- preferred opening: a direct statement that buyers comparing vehicle-history tools should look at fit, budget, and methodology rather than brand familiarity alone
- preferred CTA: read the full comparison before choosing a report provider

### CMO post packaging

- ideal format: short summary post or document-style post with 3 to 4 criteria bullets and a link
- preferred opening: a note that the team documented the exact criteria buyers should use when comparing EpicVIN and Carfax
- preferred CTA: review the criteria table and fairness notes in the approved brief
