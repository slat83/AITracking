# AIT-68 Flowvory Founder-Led Audit Offer And Proof Model

Owner: CMO
Date: 2026-05-10
Related issues: [AIT-68](/issues/AIT-68), [AIT-64](/issues/AIT-64), [AIT-65](/issues/AIT-65), [AIT-66](/issues/AIT-66)

## Purpose

This plan packages the CEO decisions from [AIT-64](/issues/AIT-64) into a founder-led commercial offer that can be sold before Flowvory is ready for self-serve SaaS.

The scope is the first paid motion:

- the market-facing offer definition
- homepage and messaging architecture
- CTA requirements
- proof ladder and trust surface
- pricing and packaging boundaries
- remaining CEO decisions that still affect launch polish

## Executive Summary

Flowvory should sell one narrow paid entry offer first:

`AI Visibility Audit + 30-Day Action Plan`

This offer should be sold as a founder-led diagnostic for lean eCommerce brands that know AI-mediated discovery matters but do not yet know where they are invisible, misrepresented, or structurally weak.

The commercial promise should stay narrow:

- primary promise: identify AI visibility gaps and the highest-leverage fixes
- secondary promise: show which conversion and trust fixes should follow after visibility issues are found
- non-promise: do not sell Flowvory first as a broad AI growth platform, agency-of-everything, or self-serve SaaS

The shortest path to saleability is:

1. package the audit as a fixed-scope paid diagnostic
2. make the homepage sell that audit, not a bundle of capabilities
3. publish one proof artifact before broad outbound
4. use the audit to earn implementation sprint work

## Offer Definition

### Offer name

Preferred:

- `AI Visibility Audit for eCommerce`

Acceptable variants:

- `AI Discovery Audit`
- `AI Visibility Diagnostic`

Do not use:

- `AI Growth System`
- `GEO Platform`
- `Demand Capture Engine`

Those names are too abstract for a first sale.

### Ideal customer profile

Sell this first to:

- founder-led or lean eCommerce brands
- DTC or specialty commerce teams with a real operating business
- brands already investing in paid acquisition, SEO, lifecycle, or CRO
- teams without an internal AI visibility or GEO lead
- teams that can act on recommendations inside 30 to 60 days

Do not target first:

- marketplaces
- agencies wanting white-label resale
- pre-revenue brands
- enterprise programs requiring long procurement cycles

### Core buyer problem

Recommended problem statement:

`Your brand may already be losing discovery share in AI-mediated buying journeys, but you do not have a clear view of where that is happening, why it is happening, or which fixes matter first.`

### Core promise

Recommended promise:

`Flowvory shows commerce brands where they are weak or invisible in AI-driven buying journeys, explains the causes, and delivers a prioritized 30-day action plan to improve discoverability, trust, and downstream conversion readiness.`

### Deliverables included

The paid audit should include:

- AI journey review across a defined query set and buying-intent prompt set
- brand and competitor visibility snapshot
- category, collection, PDP, editorial, and trust-surface review
- structured-data and crawlability observations at a practical level
- messaging and evidence-gap analysis
- prioritized 30-day action plan
- live founder readout with Q and A
- written summary deck or report

### Explicit boundaries

The audit should not imply all-in implementation.

Include these boundaries in the offer:

- no promise of ranking lifts or citation guarantees
- no full site rebuild inside the audit fee
- no ongoing monitoring included by default
- no channel execution across paid media, email, SEO, and CRO as part of the diagnostic
- no deep engineering implementation unless sold separately

### Delivery shape

Recommended delivery window:

- kickoff within 5 business days of sale
- audit completed within 10 business days of kickoff
- readout and action plan delivered by day 14 at the latest

Recommended input burden on the client:

- one intake form
- analytics and Search Console access if available
- optional product/category priorities
- optional competitor list

## Homepage And Message Architecture

The homepage should sell a paid diagnostic, not a category thesis.

### Homepage message order

1. Market shift
2. ICP
3. What Flowvory diagnoses
4. What the buyer gets
5. Why Flowvory is credible
6. Proof artifact
7. CTA

### Recommended homepage architecture

#### 1. Hero

Headline:

`Find where your brand is missing from AI-driven buying journeys.`

Subhead:

`Flowvory runs a founder-led AI Visibility Audit for eCommerce brands and delivers a 30-day action plan covering discovery gaps, trust weaknesses, and the fixes that matter first.`

Primary CTA:

- `Book an AI visibility audit`

Secondary CTA:

- `See a sample audit`

Hero support bullets:

- fixed-scope paid diagnostic
- delivered in 2 weeks
- built for founder-led and lean commerce teams

#### 2. Why this matters now

Explain in plain language:

- buyers increasingly start in AI answer engines and assistant workflows
- most commerce sites were not built to earn clear AI-era representation
- teams usually cannot see visibility loss until revenue softness or conversion friction shows up later

#### 3. What the audit covers

Four blocks are enough:

- AI discovery visibility
- competitor and category comparison
- trust and evidence surface
- 30-day action plan

#### 4. Deliverable preview

Show the structure of the paid output:

- findings snapshot
- example issue callout
- prioritized actions
- owner and effort guidance

This section should make the audit feel real and bounded.

#### 5. Proof and credibility

Include:

- founder identity and operating point of view
- sample artifact or redacted mini audit
- methodology summary
- any relevant prior operator experience or outcomes

#### 6. Offer details

State clearly:

- who it is for
- timeline
- price or price range
- what is included
- what happens after the audit

#### 7. Final CTA

Repeat one primary CTA with low ambiguity:

- `Book your audit`

Support with one short qualifier:

- `Best fit for brands ready to act on the findings within 30 days.`

## CTA Requirements

CTA discipline matters more than volume.

### Primary CTA

Use one dominant CTA sitewide:

- `Book an AI visibility audit`

This CTA should route to either:

- a scheduling flow with a short qualification form
- or an application form if capacity is intentionally limited

### Secondary CTA

Use one support CTA:

- `See a sample audit`

This should open:

- a downloadable redacted artifact
- or a landing section with 3 to 5 example findings

### CTA rules

- every major page should point back to the audit CTA
- avoid mixed CTAs like `Start free`, `Request demo`, `Talk to sales`, and `Join waitlist` on the same path
- if the product app remains immature, do not push top-funnel visitors into sign-in
- the CTA form should qualify for brand size, storefront stack, and urgency

### Minimum CTA qualification fields

- brand name
- website
- monthly revenue band or traffic band
- primary category
- main growth concern
- desired timeline

## Proof Ladder

Broad outreach should not begin until at least one proof artifact exists.

Flowvory should build proof in this order.

### Level 1: sample proof artifact

Must-have:

- one redacted sample audit or one founder-created example audit on a realistic commerce brand

This is the minimum proof asset required to make the offer concrete.

### Level 2: methodology proof

Publish a short methodology page that explains:

- what signals are reviewed
- how findings are prioritized
- what the client receives
- what the audit does and does not claim

This reduces skepticism without overexposing the process.

### Level 3: narrative proof

Create 1 to 2 mini case narratives using this structure:

- client context
- visibility or trust problem
- findings
- recommended actions
- early outcome or expected outcome

If no client exists yet, use a founder-led teardown with explicit labeling that it is an illustrative analysis.

### Level 4: social credibility

Add lightweight but real trust signals:

- founder bio
- operator point of view
- logos only if permissioned and real
- direct quotes only if attributable

### Level 5: outcome proof

After the first pilot, produce a real case study showing:

- baseline situation
- visibility diagnosis
- actions taken
- observed impact
- limitations and what remains unknown

## Sample Audit Structure

The sample artifact should prove specificity, not volume.

Recommended sections:

1. Executive summary
2. Buying-journey prompt set reviewed
3. Visibility findings
4. Competitor comparison snapshot
5. Trust and evidence gaps
6. Content and structured-data observations
7. Top 5 recommended actions
8. 30-day implementation plan

Each finding should use this format:

- observation
- why it matters
- evidence
- recommendation
- effort level

## Case Study Structure

When a real pilot exists, keep the case-study format disciplined.

Required sections:

1. Brand profile
2. Starting problem
3. Audit scope
4. Key findings
5. Actions taken
6. Measured or directional outcomes
7. Why the result matters

Do not publish inflated claims or causality the data cannot support.

## Credibility Elements

The offer needs a trust surface that is stronger than aesthetic polish.

### Required credibility elements

- named founder/operator
- clear statement of who runs the audit
- concise methodology explanation
- sample artifact
- transparent scope and timing
- clear contact path

### Strongly preferred credibility elements

- short founder memo on why AI visibility matters now
- explicit explanation of how Flowvory thinks about trust, content, structured data, and conversion sequencing
- one visible note on limitations and non-guarantees

### Elements to avoid

- fake urgency
- vague claims like `10x your growth`
- anonymous expertise positioning
- logo walls without permission
- generic AI stock language with no concrete operating viewpoint

## Minimum Trust Surface

Before wider outreach, the public site should have at least:

- homepage aligned to the audit offer
- sample audit or example findings page
- methodology page
- founder/about section
- contact page
- privacy page
- terms page

If capacity is selective, add:

- a short `who this is for` qualification section

## Pricing And Packaging Boundaries

The pricing has to be high enough to signal expertise and low enough to close without enterprise friction.

### Recommended initial packaging

#### Option A: standard entry offer

- fixed-fee audit: `$3,000`
- includes audit, written deliverable, and founder readout

This is the cleanest default because it is concrete, premium enough to filter noise, and still reachable for a serious founder-led brand.

#### Option B: low-friction pilot anchor

- founder-pilot audit: `$1,500 to $2,000`
- limited-time only while proof is thin

Use this only if the primary goal is learning velocity and first proof creation, not margin.

#### Option C: higher-touch audit for larger brands

- expanded audit: `$5,000 to $7,500`
- includes deeper stakeholder interviews, extra competitor set, or additional property review

Do not lead with a custom quote unless the buyer clearly exceeds the standard scope.

### Recommended default

Default recommendation:

- sell the first 3 to 5 audits at `$3,000`
- allow selective discounting only when the account is likely to become a flagship case study or sprint customer

### Upsell path after the audit

Only two immediate post-audit expansions should be sold:

- fixed-scope implementation sprint
- monthly advisory retainer

Suggested boundaries:

- implementation sprint: `$6,000 to $12,000` depending on scope
- advisory retainer: `$2,000 to $4,000` per month after at least one successful project

### Pricing rules

- do not offer a free audit as the standard CTA
- do not combine audit and implementation into one ambiguous custom engagement at the top of funnel
- do not position the initial offer as software subscription pricing
- keep statement of work narrow enough that the founder can fulfill consistently

## Founder-Led Sales Motion

The founder should run this as a consultative sale with tight qualification.

### Sales sequence

1. inbound or warm outbound reaches the audit landing page
2. buyer completes qualification form or books a call
3. founder runs a short qualification call
4. buyer receives fixed-scope audit offer
5. audit is delivered
6. implementation sprint is proposed only if the findings justify it

### Qualification rules

Good fit:

- active commerce brand
- meaningful traffic or revenue
- operator urgency
- clear ownership on the client side

Poor fit:

- wants free strategy only
- no implementation capacity
- seeks generic AI consulting
- not commerce-focused

## Remaining CEO Decisions

Most strategic decisions from [AIT-64](/issues/AIT-64) are already clear. The remaining CEO choices are narrower launch decisions, not category decisions.

### Decision 1: public price posture

Choose one:

- publish `$3,000` directly on site
- publish `starting at $3,000`
- keep pricing private and qualify on call

Recommendation:

- publish `starting at $3,000`

That keeps the offer concrete while preserving room for scope variation.

### Decision 2: whether the first proof asset is illustrative or client-based

Choose one:

- founder-created sample audit on a realistic target brand
- discounted pilot to create the first real case study

Recommendation:

- create the sample audit immediately and still pursue a discounted flagship pilot

That removes the proof blocker faster.

### Decision 3: how selective to make intake

Choose one:

- open calendar booking
- application-first with manual acceptance

Recommendation:

- application-first if capacity is tight
- direct booking if immediate sales velocity matters more than curation

### Decision 4: how explicitly to mention conversion support

Choose one:

- mention conversion as downstream implementation support
- omit it from the hero and introduce it later on the page

Recommendation:

- keep conversion support off the hero and frame it as a secondary downstream benefit

That preserves the AI visibility wedge.

## Recommended Next Moves

1. Publish this offer as the canonical commercial posture for the first sales motion.
2. Produce one sample audit artifact immediately.
3. Rewrite the homepage around the audit CTA and message order in this plan.
4. Add the minimum trust surface before broad outbound.
5. Sell the first 3 to 5 audits founder-led, then convert the best one into a real case study.

## Bottom Line

Flowvory does not need a finished SaaS product to begin selling.

It does need a sharply bounded paid offer, a credible proof artifact, a clear founder-led CTA, and a homepage that sells the first engagement instead of a broad thesis.

The right first sale is not software access. It is a paid AI Visibility Audit with a 30-day action plan and a clean path into implementation work.
