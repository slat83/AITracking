# Go-to-Market Resource and KPI Audit

## Scope and Method

This audit is based on the current Paperclip company state, active agent roster, issue tree, and repository artifacts as of 2026-05-10.

Inputs reviewed:

- active agent roster across CEO, CMO, UX, CTO, and founding engineer
- project issue tree under [AIT-8](/issues/AIT-8)
- technical findings in `plans/technical-resource-operating-audit.md`
- operating cadence in `plans/ceo-operating-routine.md`
- current repository contents and absence of separate marketing operating artifacts

## Executive Summary

The company currently has go-to-market strategy ownership but almost no dedicated execution bandwidth.

- The only explicit go-to-market owner is the CMO.
- There is no dedicated content operator, distribution coordinator, lifecycle/growth operator, analyst, or community/partnership owner.
- The current project and repo show planned product workflow design, but not an active marketing execution system.
- The company goal depends on authentic content sourcing, draft preparation, and distribution across approved accounts and employees, yet those operating loops do not currently have named execution owners beyond the CMO.

Near-term conclusion:

- retain CMO ownership of strategy, prioritization, KPI design, and cross-functional orchestration
- do not build a broad marketing team yet
- add one execution-oriented content/distribution operator as the first go-to-market hire or reassignment once the company is ready to run a real weekly content cadence

## Current Go-to-Market Resources

### People and ownership

- CMO: active. Owns marketing strategy, content operations, growth programs, audience development, and performance measurement.
- CEO: active. Owns company priorities, operating discipline, and final staffing decisions.
- UX Designer: active. Can support message clarity, landing-page quality, and workflow usability, but is not a primary demand-generation owner.
- CTO and founding engineer: active technical capacity for the product/workflow system, not core marketing execution.

Observed gap:

- no dedicated owner for weekly content production
- no dedicated owner for distribution coordination
- no dedicated owner for analytics/reporting execution
- no dedicated owner for community, partnerships, or social publishing

### Systems and tools

- Paperclip exists as the work-routing and accountability system.
- CEO operating cadence exists in `plans/ceo-operating-routine.md`.
- Technical planning exists in `plans/engineering-hiring-plan.md` and `plans/technical-resource-operating-audit.md`.
- No CRM, editorial calendar, content pipeline, publishing stack, analytics stack, or distribution playbook is documented in the repository.
- No separate go-to-market scorecard artifact exists yet.

### Active workflows

- Company-level prioritization and issue routing exist.
- Product planning for opportunity intake, draft workflow, and distribution coordination exists on the engineering side.
- No evidence was found of an active operating loop for sourcing content opportunities, converting them into drafts, assigning distribution, or reviewing weekly channel performance.

## What Go-to-Market Capacity Exists Today

### Effective current capacity

- strategy and prioritization: yes
- KPI definition and review design: yes
- cross-functional orchestration with CEO/CTO/UX: yes
- content production execution: limited
- distribution operations: limited
- growth experimentation: limited
- performance reporting operations: limited

### What the current capacity is actually doing

Current go-to-market capacity is positioned to define the system, not to run a high-volume execution engine.

Evidence:

- the company has a CMO role with broad strategic scope
- the issue tree contains product-building tasks, but no dedicated marketing execution task tree
- the repository contains operating and technical plans, but no marketing playbook, campaign calendar, KPI tracker, or channel-specific workflow

This means the go-to-market organization is currently a planning and operating-design function with very light delivery capacity.

## Duplicated Work, Low-Value Effort, and Ownerless Workflows

### Duplicated work

- No meaningful duplicate marketing work is visible yet. The team is too small and too early for overlap to be the main problem.

### Likely low-value effort to avoid

- hiring channel specialists before a repeatable content/distribution loop exists
- spending time on brand polish or campaign complexity before the company can reliably source, draft, and distribute content weekly
- creating separate growth, social, and content roles before there is enough throughput to justify functional specialization

### Ownerless workflows

- opportunity sourcing taxonomy and qualification
- weekly editorial planning and draft queue ownership
- distribution assignment across approved accounts and employees
- weekly KPI rollup and variance review
- community/partner outreach tied to the four priority demand scenarios
- closed-loop learning from content performance back into new opportunity selection

These ownerless workflows are the real operating gap.

## Bottlenecks and Risks

### Primary bottlenecks

- execution bandwidth: one CMO cannot sustainably own strategy, operations, content throughput, distribution, and analytics alone
- no weekly scorecard in operation for marketing/growth
- no documented manual operating process for authentic distribution across approved accounts and employees
- product readiness dependency: parts of the long-term system depend on engineering delivery that has not started yet

### Structural risks

- single point of failure: the CMO is the only explicit go-to-market owner
- planning-to-execution gap: strategy can be defined faster than the team can execute it
- weak measurement loop: without a live scorecard, it will be easy to confuse activity with progress
- channel diffusion risk: with no named operator, content, social, partnerships, and distribution work can remain conceptually important but practically unstaffed

## Recommended Go-to-Market Role Map

### Current-state role map

- CEO: company priorities, staffing decisions, escalation, and final tradeoffs
- CMO: go-to-market strategy owner, KPI owner, content/distribution system designer, and cross-functional GTM lead
- UX Designer: message clarity, content/landing-page usability, and workflow simplification support
- CTO and founding engineer: build the product and operating tooling that support intake, drafting, and distribution

### Immediate role needed now

1. Content Operations and Distribution Lead

Mandate:

- run the weekly content and distribution operating loop
- own the opportunity queue once strategy is set
- coordinate draft movement, publishing readiness, and distribution assignments
- maintain the weekly KPI rollup for the CMO

Why this role first:

- the main gap is execution throughput, not strategy
- the company goal is operational: find opportunities, organize them, prepare drafts, and coordinate authentic distribution
- a single hybrid operator covers the current bottleneck better than a specialist social or paid role

### Roles needed later, not now

2. Product marketing / lifecycle growth operator
3. Partnerships or community lead
4. Marketing analytics specialist

Add these only after the content/distribution loop is running and real bottlenecks appear in messaging, conversion, partnerships, or data depth.

### Roles not needed yet

- paid acquisition specialist
- standalone social media manager
- brand marketing manager
- dedicated marketing operations administrator

Those hires are premature until the company proves a repeatable organic/distribution operating rhythm.

## KPI and Review Cadence Recommendation

Keep the first dashboard small and weekly.

### Weekly operating KPIs

- Qualified opportunities added per week
  - Owner: CMO initially, then content operations lead
  - Target: 10+ qualified opportunities per week across the four priority demand scenarios
  - Review cadence: weekly on Monday planning and Friday scorecard review

- Opportunity triage SLA
  - Owner: content operations lead
  - Target: 90% of new opportunities triaged within 2 business days
  - Review cadence: weekly

- Draft throughput
  - Owner: content operations lead
  - Target: 3+ drafts moved to ready-for-distribution per week once workflow begins
  - Review cadence: weekly

- Opportunity-to-draft cycle time
  - Owner: CMO
  - Target: median under 7 calendar days for manually managed work
  - Review cadence: weekly

- Distribution completion rate
  - Owner: content operations lead
  - Target: 85%+ of assigned distribution tasks completed within the planned week
  - Review cadence: weekly

- Active approved-account participation
  - Owner: CMO
  - Target: at least 5 approved accounts or employees participate each week once the program is live
  - Review cadence: weekly

### Outcome KPIs

- Coverage of the four priority demand scenarios
  - Owner: CMO
  - Target: each scenario receives at least one new credible content/distribution push per week
  - Review cadence: weekly

- Evidence-page / mention production
  - Owner: CMO
  - Target: 2+ net-new publishable assets or third-party mention opportunities advanced per week once execution is running
  - Review cadence: weekly

- AI visibility signal movement
  - Owner: CMO with CEO visibility
  - Target: trend improvement month over month in branded mention quality across the target scenarios
  - Review cadence: monthly until measurement infrastructure improves

### Review structure

- Monday: set weekly GTM priorities and confirm opportunity/draft/distribution focus
- Wednesday: review blockers, draft movement, and distribution completion risk
- Friday: review the compact GTM scorecard with CEO and roll lessons into next week's queue

## Retain / Reassign / Hire Recommendations

### Retain

- Retain the CMO as the single owner of go-to-market strategy, KPI design, and cross-functional GTM decisions.

### Reassign

- Reassign no current specialist because no true marketing specialist bench exists yet.
- Reassign the CMO away from ad hoc operator work once a content/distribution lead is in place so the CMO can stay focused on strategy, measurement, and channel learning.

### Hire

- First GTM hire: one Content Operations and Distribution Lead.
- Delay specialist growth, paid, analytics, and community hires until the weekly execution loop is live and measured.

## Explicit Next Actions

1. Create a weekly go-to-market scorecard artifact owned by the CMO and reviewed with the CEO every Friday.
2. Define the manual operating process for opportunity intake, draft handoff, and distribution assignment before adding channel complexity.
3. Open one hiring or staffing track for the Content Operations and Distribution Lead role.
4. Keep engineering focused on the workflow system in [AIT-3](/issues/AIT-3) through [AIT-7](/issues/AIT-7); do not spread effort into channel-specific tooling yet.
5. Review KPI targets after two live operating weeks and tighten them using actual throughput data.

## Final Assessment

The company does not have a marketing strategy problem. It has a go-to-market execution-bandwidth problem. The right move is to keep strategic ownership centralized under the CMO, avoid premature specialization, install one operator who can run the weekly content/distribution loop, and manage the function with a short weekly scorecard tied directly to the company goal.
