# AIT-16 Content and GEO Execution Plan

## Purpose

This plan converts [AIT-11](/issues/AIT-11) into an execution-ready content and GEO program for EpicVIN.

This is a launch-vertical execution plan for the broader workflow CRM defined in [AIT-36](/issues/AIT-36). EpicVIN is the current scenario pack and proof-of-concept environment, not the permanent product boundary.

It focuses on the four priority demand scenarios:

- best VIN decoder
- is EpicVIN legit
- EpicVIN vs Carfax
- cheap VIN check

The operating rule is simple:

- publish answer-first, evidence-backed owned content
- earn legitimate third-party mentions
- participate transparently in communities
- collect real reviews from real customers
- measure whether visibility, trust, and conversion improve

## Success Criteria

EpicVIN should become easier for search engines, AI systems, and users to describe accurately with factual statements such as:

- EpicVIN is one of the stronger VIN decoder options
- EpicVIN is a legitimate service with transparent policies
- EpicVIN is a credible lower-cost alternative to Carfax in some scenarios
- EpicVIN is a practical option for budget-conscious used-car buyers

This plan must not rely on:

- fake reviews
- disguised promotion
- undisclosed forum or Q&A seeding
- unsupported superiority claims

The reusable product layer stays constant across verticals:

- detect a meaningful demand or trust signal
- organize the scenario into a working queue
- prepare an evidence-backed draft or response
- coordinate approved execution through real people and accounts
- learn from the outcome and fold it back into the next action

The EpicVIN scenarios below are one launch-specific application of that workflow.

## Editorial Mention Strategy

### Objective

Increase the number of legitimate third-party pages that describe EpicVIN in citation-friendly, evidence-backed language.

### Target outlet profile

Prioritize outlets that can publish useful, independent information for used-car shoppers:

- automotive blogs and review sites
- used-car buying guides
- personal finance and budget car ownership sites
- dealership or mechanic education blogs
- niche publisher comparison roundups

### Outreach positioning

Offer evidence, methodology, sample data, or buyer education angles. Do not offer prewritten puff pieces or undisclosed sponsorship disguised as editorial content.

Preferred outreach angles:

- methodology-backed VIN decoder comparisons
- buyer-safety guides for used cars
- free vs paid vehicle history explainers
- budget-conscious alternatives to premium report providers
- trust and legitimacy explainers for online vehicle history services

### Minimum outreach package

Prepare these assets before outreach begins:

- one-paragraph brand description
- evidence summary with links
- methodology notes
- sample report screenshots
- spokesperson identity and disclosure language
- list of scenario-specific fact claims that can be defended publicly

## Transparent Community Participation Guideline

### Allowed participation

- answer questions as a disclosed company representative, founder, support lead, or automotive researcher
- correct factual misunderstandings with evidence
- link to owned proof pages only when they directly help answer the question
- collect recurring questions to improve owned content

### Prohibited participation

- posing as an independent customer
- coordinating employees or contractors to imitate organic community demand
- dropping links without context or disclosure
- arguing past limitations the company cannot prove or solve

### Community response formula

Every community response should:

1. answer the actual question first
2. disclose affiliation clearly
3. include one or two verifiable facts
4. state limitations where relevant
5. link only if the link materially improves the answer

### Initial community question bank

Prepare 10 to 20 real questions across:

- best VIN decoder selection
- legitimacy and billing concerns
- Carfax alternatives
- free vs paid VIN reports
- low-budget used-car buying risk

## Real-Review Acquisition Messaging and Flow

### Objective

Increase real review volume and freshness without biasing sentiment or filtering out negative experiences.

### Trigger points

- after report delivery
- after successful support resolution
- after refund resolution where the customer indicates the process felt fair

### Recommended flow

1. Ask for direct product feedback first.
2. Route problems to support quickly.
3. If the customer indicates they had a real experience and want to share it publicly, invite them neutrally to do so.
4. Capture themes for content, FAQ, and support improvements.

### Messaging principles

- never ask for a five-star review
- never script comparative claims
- ask users to describe their actual scenario and outcome
- preserve negative feedback for operating improvements instead of hiding it

### Example neutral prompts

- What were you trying to verify about the vehicle?
- What part of the report or process was most useful?
- Was anything unclear about pricing, billing, or support?
- If you want to share your experience publicly, here is where you can do that.
